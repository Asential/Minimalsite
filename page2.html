List of My Top 5 Games

Bioshock
Bioshock - the first installment - is nothing short of jaw-dropping in its carefully remastered new form. Returning now is to experience it entirely anew, to feel the sudden intake of breath as you first break the water's surface after the opening's devastating plane crash and take glimpse of the awaiting vista; the towering flames erupting from the wreckage only to be engulfed by the rich silk of the night, and looming out of the darkness - a lighthouse. Always a lighthouse.
  • First Person Shooter
  • Atmoshpheric Horror
  • Amazing Story
  • Great Game Mechanics
The Elder Scroll V: Skyrim
Like every great RPG, the fifth Elder Scrolls game is a grand enterprise. It’s huge in scale, epic in its breadth and scope, and will occupy such vast quantities of your time that you may find yourself losing sleep, dodging work and testing the patience of family and friends while you play it. Most of all, it’s a game destined to cast a shadow over the rest of the genre for several years to come, just as The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion did before it.
  • Third Person
  • Role-Playing Game
  • Amazing Story
  • Amazing Game Mechanics
  • Lots to Do
The Witcher: The Wild Hunt
The Witcher 3 is as dense and deep as the other two games in the series in terms of RPG mechanics, and the overwhelmingly massive open-world environment has at once made that depth more intimidating, and in the long run, more rewarding.
  • Third Person
  • Role-Playing Game
  • Breathtaking Story
  • Visuals ahead of its time
  • Great variety
Half Life 2
In my eyes, half life 2 is one of the best games ever made. Many of the concepts in the game aren't original. However, it was the first game to bring all of them together in a completely unique and effective way. Half life 2 paves the road for modern first person shooters in every way imaginable.
  • First Person Shooter
  • Thriller
  • Amazing Story
  • Ahead of It's time
Call of Duty 4: Modern Warfare
It's a breathtaking moment in a campaign filled with breathtaking moments. Unfortunately, it's about half as long as the average shooter, and there are plenty of sequences where you wish there were just one or two more hills to take. Of course, if you're looking for longevity, that's where the multiplayer comes in.
  • First Person Shooter
  • Action
  • Great Story
  • Great Game mechanics
